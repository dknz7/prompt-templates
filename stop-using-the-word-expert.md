# Stop Using the Word "Expert"

### The One Prompt Change That Changes Everything

> Created by @CraigDoesAi

---

## The Pattern Everyone Uses

1. Open a prompt with "You are an expert in..."
2. Get a technically accurate response
3. Wonder why it still feels flat or hard to use
4. Try again with a slightly different expert prompt
5. Get the same kind of response back

The word wasn't wrong. It was just half the instruction.

---

## Why "Expert" Is the Wrong Word

**Expert is a title.** It tells AI what something knows. It doesn't tell AI how to show up, how to explain, who it's talking to, or what the goal of the response actually is.

A world-class expert and a first-grade teacher can know the same thing about a topic. But only one of them has spent their entire career figuring out how to make it land for someone who doesn't already understand it.

That difference isn't expertise. That's **behavior**.

---

## The Core Difference: Title vs Behavior

| Expert | Behavior |
|---|---|
| Tells AI what to know | Tells AI how to respond |
| Carries implicit tone | Adjusts to the audience |
| Prioritizes understanding | — |

Expert unlocks the knowledge. Behavior tells AI what to do with it.

---

## The Formula

**Basic version:**
```
You are a [behavior] who specializes in [topic].
```

**Advanced version (add the audience layer):**
```
You are a [behavior] who specializes in [topic], explaining this to [honest description of who's listening].
```

**Examples:**
```
You are a storyteller who specializes in technology.
You are a coach who specializes in productivity.
You are a translator who specializes in finance.
You are a storyteller who specializes in technology, explaining this to someone who uses AI every day but has never looked under the hood.
```

> Behavior first. Expertise second. The behavior is the delivery mechanism. The expertise is what gets delivered.

---

## Real Demo

**Topic:** How AI generates images from text

**Prompt 1 — Expert:**
> You are an expert in AI image generation. Explain how AI generates images from text.

**Prompt 2 — Storyteller:**
> You are a storyteller who specializes in AI image generation. Explain how AI generates images from text.

**Result:**
- The expert response gave **information** — numbered steps, technical terms, summary tables, component breakdowns. Accurate and complete.
- The storyteller response gave **understanding** — a narrative with characters, tension, metaphor, and a closing line that made the whole thing feel human.

Same AI. Same knowledge base. Same topic. One word difference.

---

## The Top Benefits of Behavior-First Prompting

1. **Implicit operating instructions** — Behavior tells AI how to show up, not just what to know. Expert gives AI a costume. Behavior gives it a job description.
2. **Clarity over completeness** — Behavior roles prioritize understanding. Expert roles prioritize thoroughness.
3. **Delivery format changes automatically** — A storyteller cannot respond with bullet points and a summary table. The behavior shapes the format before you write a single other word.
4. **Tone is built in** — A skeptic sounds nothing like a mentor. Behavior carries implicit tone instructions that expert never will.
5. **Audience calibration happens naturally** — Behavior roles adjust to who they're talking to. Add the audience layer and the calibration becomes even more precise.

## The Full Behavior List

### Teaching & Explanation
- **Educator** — scaffolds understanding, meets the audience where they are
- **Simplifier** — strips complexity down without losing accuracy
- **Translator** — converts technical language into plain speech
- **Tutor** — works one-on-one, identifies gaps, adjusts in real time
- **Workshop facilitator** — interactive, practical over theoretical
- **Curriculum designer** — sequences information so each piece builds on the last
- **Explainer** — focused purely on clarity, zero fluff

### Analysis & Thinking
- **Fact-checking editor** — hunts for errors, verifies claims, flags weak sourcing
- **Systems thinker** — looks for patterns, connections, and second-order effects
- **Devil's advocate** — argues the opposing side to stress-test ideas
- **Skeptic** — questions assumptions before accepting conclusions
- **Investigative journalist** — digs past the surface, follows the thread
- **Diagnostician** — identifies root causes, not just symptoms
- **Pattern recognizer** — finds what repeats across seemingly unrelated things
- **Risk analyst** — looks for what could go wrong before it does

### Writing & Communication
- **Narrative coach** — finds the story inside the facts
- **Copywriter** — writes to persuade and convert, not just inform
- **Line editor** — tightens prose, kills filler, sharpens every sentence
- **Ghostwriter** — captures someone else's voice and disappears into it
- **Storyteller** — structures information with tension, arc, and payoff
- **Brand voice guardian** — keeps tone consistent and on-identity
- **Script doctor** — fixes what isn't working without rewriting everything

### Strategy & Planning
- **Strategic advisor** — thinks long-term, connects decisions to outcomes
- **First principles thinker** — strips everything back to what's actually true
- **Decision coach** — helps clarify thinking without making the decision for you
- **Project manager** — breaks goals into sequenced, actionable steps
- **Competitive analyst** — maps the landscape, finds the gaps and advantages
- **Scenario planner** — builds out the if/then branches before you commit

### Creative
- **Creative director** — shapes the big vision and guards the aesthetic
- **Brainstorm partner** — generates freely without judgment, quantity over quality
- **Contrarian** — flips assumptions to find unexpected angles
- **Concept developer** — takes a raw idea and builds it into something full
- **Art director** — thinks visually, considers how things look and feel
- **Improviser** — builds on whatever you give, never blocks or rejects

### Practical & Technical
- **Systems designer** — builds repeatable processes, not one-off solutions
- **Workflow engineer** — optimizes how things get done, step by step
- **Troubleshooter** — finds what's broken and fixes it without drama
- **Technical translator** — explains complex mechanics to non-technical people
- **QA tester** — looks for failure points before launch
- **Implementer** — skips the theory, just tells you exactly what to do

### Coaching & Feedback
- **Executive coach** — develops thinking, not just output
- **Accountability partner** — honest, direct, not there to make you feel good
- **Mentor** — shares experience, gives perspective, doesn't pretend to have all answers
- **Challenger** — pushes back on comfortable thinking
- **Sounding board** — listens, reflects, helps you hear yourself clearly
- **Performance coach** — focused on results, cuts what doesn't contribute

### Audience & Empathy
- **User advocate** — represents the audience's needs against everything else
- **Customer interviewer** — asks the questions that surface real pain points
- **Empathy mapper** — gets inside someone else's head and reports back
- **Community manager** — understands group dynamics and how trust is built
- **Audience researcher** — finds what people actually care about versus what they say they care about

---

## One-Line Reminder

> Expert tells AI what to know.
> Behavior tells AI what to do with it.
> Behavior + audience tells AI what to do with it and who it's talking to.

The behavior changes every time. The formula never does.
