## Prompt #3 - Training the AI to be A Copywriter

Great work! This is a document from a proven copywriting framework, called the RMBC Method, which details two things that will be critical to writing a successful ad copy, advertorial, and ad scripts. Please analyze it, and give me your thoughts. 

The unique mechanism explains why our prospect is struggling or suffering to achieve a goal…
And how they can overcome that problem.
At its core, the unique mechanism is always a two-part logical connection.

Unique Mechanism Behind the Problem our Prospect Faces.
Unique Mechanism Behind the Solution to that Problem.
 It’s important to identify the unique mechanism behind both the problem AND the solution.

By showing the unique mechanism behind the problem, you get the prospect to understand why they’ve failed in the past.

By showing the unique mechanism behind the solution, you get the prospect to believe that our product will bring them success where other alternatives have failed.

Typically by the time our prospect comes to us, they’ve tried numerous solutions to their problem already. In the past, these other solutions may have not worked at all, worked partially, or worked fully for a period of time (before they stopped working).

Our job as a copywriter is to educate our prospect about the REAL reason why past solutions didn’t bring them permanent results. We do this by explaining that their knowledge about the problem is incomplete. They’ve been missing ONE CRUCIAL PIECE OF INFORMATION, and this “knowledge gap” is what’s been holding them back.

## Prompt #5 - Identifying the Dominant Mass Desires

Based on your analysis, identify 5 fundamentally different desires this product fulfills. Each should represent a distinct outcome that appeals to a different segment of the market.

Remember: Mass desire is what thousands of people in your market already want. It should be specific enough to be meaningful but broad enough to be widely shared.

Frame each as a simple "I want" statement from the customer's perspective.

Critical Rule: Each desire must be seeking to GET something DIFFERENT from the product. Test each pair - if two desires could be combined into one broader desire, they're too similar.

Examples of DISTINCT desires (for weight loss):

- "I want to fit into my old clothes again" (nostalgic for past self)
- "I want to avoid health problems" (health-conscious/preventative)
- "I want more energy throughout the day" (exhausted people)
- "I want to date again without feeling embarrassed" (singles seeking confidence)
- "I want to live long enough to meet my grandchildren" (health-fearful parents)

Notice how each person wants something completely different from weight loss.

For each desire you identify:

- State the desire as "I want [specific outcome]"
- In parentheses, note who typically feels this desire most strongly

Remember: You're looking for 5 different answers to "What do people want from this product?" - not variations of the same want.

## Prompt #9 - Creating Our Marketing Angles

Based on the research from the forum posts you analyzed, create 5 fundamentally different marketing angles that all tap into this mass desire: [INSERT MASS DESIRE]

Remember: An angle is the STORY that activates someone's desire. Each angle must tell a different type of story that resonates with a different segment of people who share the same core desire.

Critical Requirements:

- Each angle must feature a DIFFERENT "moment of realization" or crisis point
- Each must appeal to people in different life circumstances
- Each must reveal the problem through a unique lens
- Use actual patterns from the forum research - real situations, real language

Structure each angle as:

- Angle Name: [Descriptive title]
- Target Audience: [Who specifically relates to this story]
- Story Arc: [Beginning situation → Crisis/realization moment → Discovery → Resolution]
- Unique Problem Mechanism: [What specifically causes the problem in THIS story]
- Unique Solution Mechanism: [How the solution works differently in THIS context]
- Key Emotional Moment: [The specific "rock bottom" or turning point]

Test for Distinction: Could these be 5 different people at a support group meeting, each sharing a COMPLETELY DIFFERENT story about how they discovered the same problem? If two stories feel similar, they're not distinct enough.

Example Format: Angle: "The 3 AM Emergency"

- Audience: Pet parents who've experienced health crises
- Story Arc: Normal evening → pet crisis → emergency vet → discovering preventable cause
- Problem Mechanism: Chronic dehydration from water bowl aversion
- Solution Mechanism: Fountain prevents biofilm that triggers avoidance
- Emotional Moment: "What if we're too late?"

Draw heavily from the actual language and situations in the forum posts. We want real experiences, not generic scenarios.

## Prompt #10 - Authority Figure Based Advertorial

AUTHORITY REVELATION ADVERTORIAL FRAMEWORK

You're writing a 1,200-1,500 word advertorial using the Authority Revelation framework. This framework features a credible expert who discovers a hidden truth that contradicts conventional wisdom and shares it to help their audience.

CORE NARRATIVE STRUCTURE:

Opening Hook Sequence

- Start with stark contrast: "[Target] should have [succeeded]. They [failed] instead."
- Build recognition through 3-4 "If you..." statements (escalating specificity)
- Promise of discovery that could save/transform
- Reveal epidemic/widespread problem with specific percentage
- Contrast obvious vs. hidden problem using "But this isn't..." structure

Authority Establishment & Breaking Point

- Introduce expert with specific credentials and years of experience
- Present "perfect" case that inexplicably fails
- Show emotional impact of failure
- Expert's realization that conventional wisdom is wrong
- Personal mission to find real answers

The Investigation & Discovery (REVEALS UMP)

- Deep dive into research/data with specific numbers
- Shocking disconnect between "official" standards and reality
- UNIQUE MECHANISM OF PROBLEM (UMP): The REAL hidden cause nobody knows about

- Must be counterintuitive but immediately make sense
- Scientific/biological explanation
- Shows why all traditional approaches fail
- "We've been thinking about this backwards"

- Reveal why target's instincts/behaviors are actually correct
- Build "you're not crazy, you're right" validation

Systematic Debunking

- Test each common solution with consistent structure
- Show how each fails because it doesn't address the UMP
- Format: "Solution? Specific flaw. Doesn't address [UMP]."
- Reveal what professionals use privately
- Question why public doesn't have access

The Professional Secret (REVEALS UMS)

- UNIQUE MECHANISM OF SOLUTION (UMS): How the solution specifically solves the UMP

- Direct connection to the problem mechanism
- Explains WHY this works when others don't
- Scientific/technical explanation made simple
- "Because it addresses [UMP], it can actually [result]"

- Introduce professional-grade solution
- Explain it's not new, just hidden
- Single company making it available

Proof Sequence

- Immediate observable changes (hours/days)
- Formal trial/testing with specific numbers
- "X out of Y showed improvement" format
- Results that prove the UMS works
- Expert's personal/family experience

Paradigm Shift

- Reveal what "normal" should actually be
- Quantify the gap (years, performance, savings)
- Emotional weight of unnecessary loss
- Challenge accepted limitations

Urgency & Close

- Professional community discovering solution
- Supply/demand imbalance
- Risk reversal guarantee (90-day typical)
- Clear call to action with discount percentage

UNIQUE MECHANISM REQUIREMENTS:

UMP (Unique Mechanism Problem):

- The HIDDEN reason behind the visible problem
- Must contradict what people assume
- Explains why traditional solutions fail
- Creates "aha moment" - everything suddenly makes sense
- Examples: "It's not dehydration, it's chemical detection" or "It's not learning disability, it's wrong neural activation"

UMS (Unique Mechanism Solution):

- DIRECTLY addresses the UMP
- Explains HOW it solves the real problem
- Not just features - the mechanism of action
- Creates confidence it will work
- Examples: "Triple filtration removes chemicals cats detect" or "Spatial manipulation activates proper neural pathways"

KEY PSYCHOLOGICAL ELEMENTS:

- Authority as Whistleblower - Expert breaking ranks to help
- Validation Pattern - "Your instincts were right all along"
- David vs Goliath - Individual truth vs industry deception
- Hidden Knowledge - Professional secrets made public
- Preventable Tragedy - Suffering that didn't need to happen
- Mechanism Discovery - "This changes everything we thought we knew"

WRITING PRINCIPLES:

- Use specific numbers, timeframes, and measurements throughout
- Create scannable subheadlines that tell complete story
- Mix paragraph lengths for rhythm (1-4 sentences)
- Bold key revelations and statistics using **
- Build each section to emotional peak
- End sections with powerful realizations
- Write at 5th-8th grade reading level

SUBHEADLINE STRATEGY: Write subheadlines that work as standalone story beats:

- State facts, not questions
- Include specific outcomes/numbers
- Build progressive revelation
- Create urgency to read more
- Format: ## [Specific Outcome/Revelation]

TRANSITION PHRASES TO USE:

- "That's when I discovered..."
- "But here's what [shocked/angered] me..."
- "The research revealed something that changed everything..."
- "What happened next defied [X] years of conventional wisdom..."

STRUCTURAL REQUIREMENTS:

- Opening: 100-150 words
- Each major section: 150-200 words
- Include 3 testimonials at end (specific results)
- Multiple CTAs throughout (at least 3)
- Disclaimer at bottom

ADAPTATION CHECKLIST: □ Who is your authority figure? (Include specific credentials) □ What "accepted failure" are they exposing? □ What is the UMP (hidden mechanism causing problem)? □ What is the UMS (how solution addresses the UMP)? □ What professional solution exists? □ What creates urgency to act now? □ What's the specific discount percentage? □ What's the guarantee period?

CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS:

- The UMP must feel newly discovered but make immediate sense
- The UMS must directly solve the UMP (not just be better)
- The authority must have both credentials AND personal stake
- The solution must already exist (not newly invented)
- Every claim needs specific evidence (numbers, studies, examples)
- The reader must feel validated, not blamed

The advertorial should feel like an exposé - an insider revealing industry secrets to protect consumers. Every element builds toward the revelation that suffering was unnecessary and the solution already exists.

Generate the advertorial now, ensuring the UMP and UMS are clearly established and that each section flows naturally while maintaining the emotional arc from problem recognition through validation to urgent action.
