# The Surviving Answer Method
> A 5-step sequence that forces AI to earn the final answer.

**Source:** [@CraigDoesAI](https://youtube.com/@craigdoesai) · [Watch the video](https://youtu.be/k1dNyhqfsQ8)

---

## Overview

Most AI answers are *safe* answers — not strong ones. This sequence puts every response under real pressure and only keeps what survives. The result is a final answer that's filtered, tested, and built around a standard you actually defined.

| Use it for | Skip it for |
|---|---|
| Judgment calls — offers, positioning, strategy, titles, decisions, scripts | Simple factual tasks, summaries, straightforward requests |

---

## The 5 Steps at a Glance

| # | Step | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Answer** | Get the strongest first response |
| 2 | **Oppose** | Force AI to challenge it |
| 3 | **Sort** | Separate solid from shaky |
| 4 | **Decide** | Define what "better" actually means |
| 5 | **Rebuild** | Reconstruct using only what survived |

---

## Step 1 — Answer
*Get the strongest first response*

Give AI real structure — your topic, your goal, your context, your constraints. Ask it to rank ideas and explain why each one has potential. This is your raw material, not your final answer.

**Template:**
```
Give me your strongest ideas for [TOPIC/PROBLEM].

Goal: [WHAT YOU'RE TRYING TO ACHIEVE]
Audience/Context: [WHO THIS IS FOR OR YOUR SITUATION]
Constraints: [WHAT LIMITATIONS APPLY]

Rank the ideas from strongest to weakest and briefly explain why each one has potential.
```

> [!example]- Example Prompt
> Give me your strongest ideas for a digital product I could sell online.
> 
> Goal: Ideas that are practical, useful, and realistic for one person to create.  
> Audience: A solo creator with useful knowledge or experience who wants to make something people would actually buy.  
> Constraints: Must be buildable by one person, no big team, no startup funding required. Must solve a real problem.
> 
> Rank the ideas from strongest to weakest and briefly explain why each one has potential.

---

## Step 2 — Oppose
*Force AI to attack its own answer*

This is where the method starts earning its name. You're not asking for more ideas — you're applying pressure to the ones you already have. Weak ideas that sounded good in the first pass will start showing cracks here.

**Template:**
```
Now take the strongest opposing point of view and challenge those ideas.

Point out which ones may be too generic, too saturated, too hard to sell, 
too time-consuming to build, unclear in value, or likely to fail for 
[YOUR SITUATION]. Be specific.
```

> [!example]- Example Prompt
> Now take the strongest opposing point of view and challenge those ideas.
> 
> Point out which ones may be too generic, too saturated, too hard to sell, too time-consuming to build, unclear in value, or likely to fail for a solo creator. Be specific.

---

## Step 3 — Sort
*Turn the mess into a map*

Now you have ideas and critiques — but they're unorganised. This step creates structure so you can see what's worth keeping and what needs to go.

**Template:**
```
Now sort the strongest ideas and critiques into three groups:

1. Well-grounded and solid
2. Promising but unproven
3. Weak assumptions, weak logic, or likely failure points

Then identify which ideas — or parts of ideas — still hold up after the critique.
```

> [!example]- Example Prompt
> Now sort the strongest ideas and critiques into three groups:
> 
> 1. Well-grounded and solid  
> 2. Promising but unproven  
> 3. Weak assumptions, weak logic, or likely failure points
> 
> Then identify which digital product ideas — or parts of ideas — still hold up after the critique.

---

## Step 4 — Decide
*The step almost nobody takes — don't skip this one*

> [!warning] Don't skip this step
> Without it, AI will try to be everything at once and give you a balanced, safe, generic final answer. This is where you choose the lens.

**Template:**
```
Before rebuilding the final answer, decide what standard matters most for this situation.

Choose the single most important standard from the following:
Practicality / Demand / Ease of Creation / Low Risk / Profit Potential / Long-Term Value

Tell me which one matters most for [YOUR SITUATION] and why.
```

**Available standards:**

| Standard | When to pick it |
|---|---|
| Practicality | You need something that works in the real world now |
| Demand | Market size and audience hunger matter most |
| Ease of Creation | Speed to ship is the priority |
| Low Risk | Downside protection is critical |
| Profit Potential | Revenue ceiling is the goal |
| Long-Term Value | Compounding returns over time matter most |

> [!example]- Example Prompt
> Before rebuilding the final answer, decide what standard matters most for this situation.
> 
> Choose the single most important standard from the following:  
> Practicality / Demand / Ease of Creation / Low Risk / Profit Potential / Long-Term Value
> 
> Tell me which one matters most for a solo creator building their first digital product and why.

---

## Step 5 — Rebuild
*Reconstruct using only what survived*

This is the payoff. You're not looking at the first answer anymore. You're looking at something that went through generation, critique, sorting, and a defined decision standard — and came out the other side sharper for it.

**Template:**
```
Rebuild the final answer using only the ideas or elements that survived the critique.

Make sure the final answer follows the decision rule you chose.

Then briefly stress-test the final answer — what could still make it fail, 
underperform, or break down in the real world?
```

> [!example]- Example Prompt
> Rebuild the final answer using only the digital product ideas or elements that survived the critique.
> 
> Make sure the final answer follows the decision rule you chose — ease of creation.
> 
> Then briefly stress-test the final answer — what could still make it fail, underperform, or break down in the real world for a solo creator?

---

## The Repair Move
*For when the final answer is close but not quite there*

Don't regenerate. Don't start over. Just send this and let AI do surgical repair on only the weak part.

```
Run the Surviving Answer Method again — but only fix what failed. Then rebuild.
```

