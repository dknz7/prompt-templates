# Research Prompts

**Competitor Analysis:** "You're an expert in market research. I'll provide the names of three competitors in my niche. Compare their strengths, weaknesses, pricing, and customer feedback. Include 3–5 action steps I can take to outshine them based on your analysis."

**Industry Trends:** "Act as a market analyst. I want you to gather current trends in [Insert Industry]. Identify any emerging patterns, technologies, or consumer behaviors. Then outline at least 3 specific ways these shifts might affect my business strategy."

**Consumer Insight:** "Serve as a consumer behavior specialist. Given my audience demographics (e.g., age range, profession, or interests), pinpoint 5 key motivations or pain points they experience. Suggest methods to address these in my upcoming products or marketing campaigns."

**Pricing Exploration:** "Assume the role of a business consultant. Investigate how similar brands set their prices for products or services in my niche. Provide a summary of typical pricing structures, value propositions, and any premium or budget positioning. Then recommend a potential pricing approach for my offering."

**Survey Summary:** "You're a data analyst. I'll provide a raw dataset or survey results. Summarize the key findings in plain language, focusing on patterns or standout responses. Provide 2–3 actionable recommendations based on the insights."

**Geographic Market Research:** "Act as a localization expert. Investigate the demand for [Insert Product/Service] in [Insert City/Region]. Highlight any cultural, economic, or competitive factors unique to this area. Conclude with 3 practical steps I should consider before entering that market."

**Topic Deep Dive:** "You're an expert researcher. I want detailed background information on [Insert Topic], including historical context, major developments, and current debates. Emphasize clarity and avoid jargon. End with a short list of reliable sources or references."

**Future Outlook:** "Serve as a futurist. Explore potential directions my field could take in the next 5 years. Pinpoint 2–3 emerging opportunities or challenges, and suggest how a small business or individual might prepare for them now."
